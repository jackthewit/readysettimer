Free Online Timer · Pomodoro · Stopwatch

ReadySetTimer is a free browser-based timer designed for presenters, students, runners, and anyone who needs a clean countdown without ads clogging the screen. Runs instantly — no sign-up, no download — with presentation-scale digits, a built-in Pomodoro, and a stopwatch that exports to CSV.

What makes ReadySetTimer different

  • Stage-visible digits — huge, tabular-number display with a circular progress ring, visible from the back of a lecture hall.
  • Color-shifting background — cyan → amber → red as the countdown narrows, so you feel time pass without looking.
  • Final 6-second big countdown — a screen-filling number replaces the UI at the finish for dramatic timing.
  • Pomodoro automation — 25 min focus + 5 min break + 15 min long break every 4 cycles, all auto-advancing.
  • Stopwatch with memos — tag each lap with a name (e.g. "Alice", "Rep 3"), save the session with a title and note, and export as CSV.
  • Fullscreen focus mode — press F to hide all chrome; controls reappear on hover.
  • No account, no tracking, no server — all your settings and records live only in your browser.

Who it's for

  • Speakers & educators — presentation countdowns, lecture segments, debate clocks.
  • Focus-block students — Pomodoro sessions for deep work, essay drafts, exam prep.
  • Cooks — quick 3-, 5-, 10-, 20-minute reminders without opening a separate app.
  • Runners & coaches — interval training, team relays, split analysis with CSV export.

Frequently asked questions

Is ReadySetTimer really free?

Yes — completely free. No sign-up, no payment, no app install. It runs in your browser and is supported by non-intrusive ads.

How do I use Pomodoro?

Select the "Pomodoro" tab, press Space or the start button. The app runs 25 minutes of focus, then auto-advances to a 5-minute break, and gives you a 15-minute long break after every 4 cycles. You can customize those minutes in Settings.

Can I bookmark a 5-minute or 25-minute timer directly?

Yes. Use URL parameters: rstimer.kr/?t=5m, rstimer.kr/?t=25m, rstimer.kr/?t=1h30m. The app opens with that preset pre-selected.

Does it work offline?

Once loaded, the timer keeps running without a network connection. Reloading the page requires internet (the HTML/CSS/JS must be fetched once).

Can I export my stopwatch records?

Yes. Each saved session has an "Export CSV" button. The file is UTF-8 with a BOM so Excel and Google Sheets open it correctly, including Korean / Japanese / Chinese memos.

Which browsers are supported?

Any modern browser: Chrome, Safari, Firefox, Edge, on desktop or mobile. Uses standard web APIs (requestAnimationFrame, performance.now, AudioContext, localStorage, dialog).
